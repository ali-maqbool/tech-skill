The best digital skill is not the one that sounds impressive. It is the one you can practise consistently, turn into visible work, and explain clearly to another person.

## Start with the outcome you want

Before choosing a course, name the result you want. Do you want to design brands, build websites, market a business, edit videos, or work independently? A clear outcome makes the learning path much easier to follow.

## Build proof while you learn

Employers and clients respond to proof. Small, complete projects teach more than endless tutorials because they give you something to improve, discuss, and show.

> Your portfolio is not a final exam. It is evidence that you can create value.

## Keep the next step small

Choose one project, one deadline, and one useful piece of feedback. Momentum comes from finishing and improving—not from collecting more tabs, certificates, or tools.
